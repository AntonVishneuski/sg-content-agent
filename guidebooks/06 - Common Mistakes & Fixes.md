## The Diagnostic Framework

When a selling post isn't working, ask these questions in order:

1. Did people stop to read/watch? → Hook problem
2. Did people read but not engage? → Content or relevance problem
3. Did people engage but not click? → CTA problem
4. Did people click but not convert? → Offer or trust problem

Most problems fall into one of the six categories below.

---

## Mistake 1: Shameless Promotion (Overselling)

**What it looks like:**
- Every post is about your product, service, or offer
- Content reads like an ad
- Audience starts ignoring your posts or unfollowing

**Why it fails:**
- People come to social media for entertainment, education, and connection — not advertisements
- Constant pitching signals low trust and desperation
- Algorithms deprioritize content with low organic engagement

**The Fix:**
Apply the 80/20 or 50/30/20 content rule immediately.

```
New content ratio:
→ 80% value (education, inspiration, entertainment)
→ 20% promotional (offers, CTAs to buy)

Or more granularly:
→ 50% educational/engaging (your original content)
→ 30% curated industry content
→ 20% promotional
```

Audit your last 10 posts. If more than 2 are direct promotions, you have a ratio problem.

---

## Mistake 2: Assuming Buyer Awareness

**What it looks like:**
- Posts go straight to pitching the product to cold audiences
- Content assumes people already know they have the problem you solve
- Using insider language that only "product aware" people understand

**Why it fails:**
- Most of your audience is at Stage 1 (Unaware) or Stage 2 (Problem Aware)
- A hard pitch to an unaware audience is irrelevant noise
- Mismatch between content and awareness stage kills trust

**The Fix:**
Map your content to the [[02 - Buyer Awareness Framework]].

| Your audience stage | Use this content type |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| Unaware | Story-led, relatable scenarios |
| Problem Aware | Educational breakdowns |
| Solution Aware | Comparisons, demonstrations |
| Product Aware | Case studies, testimonials |
| Most Aware | Direct offers, urgency posts |

Before writing, ask: "Where is my reader on this journey?"

---

## Mistake 3: Missing the Call to Action

**What it looks like:**
- Posts end with no instruction for what to do next
- Assuming readers will naturally reach out
- Vague endings like "let me know what you think"

**Why it fails:**
> Assumption is the enemy of conversion.

People will not take action unless they are told clearly what action to take and why to take it now.

**The Fix:**
Every post needs an explicit CTA. No exceptions.

```
For educational posts (Stages 1-3):
→ "Save this for later"
→ "Comment [KEYWORD] for the full guide"
→ "Follow for more strategies like this"

For conversion posts (Stage 5):
→ "Click the link in bio to book your call"
→ "DM me [WORD] and I'll share the details"
→ "Spots close [date] — link below"
```

The CTA must include:
- **What to do** (specific action)
- **Why do it** (what they get)
- **When** (urgency, where relevant)

## Mistake 4: Chasing Broad Virality

**What it looks like:**
- Creating content designed to appeal to everyone
- Posting about trending topics unrelated to your niche
- Celebrating high view counts from the wrong audience

**Why it fails:**
- Broad content attracts "freeloaders" — people who consume but never buy
- Random viral content confuses the algorithm about who your audience is
- Millions of irrelevant views = zero new clients

**The Fix:**
Shift the metric of success from views to **qualified engagement**.

```
Old goal: "Go viral"
New goal: "Get on-target virality"
```

- Write content that would mean nothing to most people — but everything to your ideal customer
- Niche-specific pain-point posts outperform general relatable content for business results
- A post seen by 500 ideal buyers beats one seen by 500,000 random scrollers

---

## Mistake 5: Platform-Hopping and Cross-Promotion

**What it looks like:**
- Sharing YouTube links on Instagram
- Posting "follow me on TikTok" from LinkedIn
- Trying to build an audience on 5 platforms simultaneously

**Why it fails:**
- Platforms algorithmically **penalize** posts that push users to external links
- Most platforms want to keep users on their platform — they suppress external link posts
- Trying to be everywhere produces mediocre presence everywhere

**The Fix:**
Treat each platform as a **separate island**.

```
Rule: Never use one platform to redirect to another platform.

Instead: Drive all platforms toward OWNED assets (email list).
→ Instagram → Lead magnet opt-in page → Email list
→ YouTube → Free guide → Email list
→ LinkedIn → Newsletter → Email list
```

Master one platform for 6 months. Then expand — not by cross-promoting, but by repurposing content natively to each new platform.

## Mistake 6: Skipping the Relationship Stage

**What it looks like:**
- DMing new followers immediately with a sales pitch
- Sending cold outreach offers to strangers
- Running ads to cold audiences with hard CTAs

**Why it fails:**
- Social media relationships mirror real relationships — you don't propose on the first date
- Cold audiences have zero trust, zero context, and zero reason to buy
- Hard pitches to cold leads damage your brand reputation

**The Fix:**
Build the Know → Like → Trust ladder **before** asking for the sale.

```
The right sequence:
1. Publish value-driven content (Know stage)
2. Engage authentically in comments (Like stage)
3. Share results, stories, case studies (Trust stage)
4. Invite warm audience to next step (Conversion)
```

For cold audiences in your DMs:
- Start with a genuine question or observation
- Offer a free resource relevant to their situation
- Let them experience your value before introducing your offer

## Mistake 7: Production Overload

**What it looks like:**
- Spending weeks perfecting high-production videos before posting
- Believing you need professional lighting, editing, and cameras to be taken seriously
- Using production quality as an excuse not to publish

**Why it fails:**
- For expertise-based businesses, clients pay for your knowledge — not your cinematography
- Audiences trust authenticity over polish
- Content that never gets published converts at exactly 0%

**The Fix:**
Prioritize publishing over perfecting.

> "A valuable insight in a noisy environment beats a perfect video no one sees."

Start with:
- Your phone camera, natural lighting, clear audio
- Simple screen-recordings or talking-head videos
- Text-based posts on LinkedIn or Instagram

Add production quality only once your core message is resonating and you have a consistent audience.

## A Note on Paid Advertising

Paid ads are mentioned here as a mistake context (running hard CTAs to cold audiences). But ads aren't inherently wrong — only the pairing is wrong.

**When ads work:**
- Retargeting warm audiences who already engaged with your organic content
- Promoting a lead magnet to a lookalike audience (built from existing buyers)
- Amplifying an organic post that is already converting well

**When ads fail:**
- Cold traffic → hard sell (no trust, no context)
- Ads sent to people who have never heard of you and are asked to buy immediately
- Using ads as a substitute for organic trust-building

> **Rule of thumb:** Ads accelerate what's already working organically. They do not fix a broken offer or a mismatched audience.

---

## Quick Diagnostic Checklist

Run every selling post through this before publishing:

- [ ] Is the hook a story or provocative question — not a generic statement?
- [ ] Is this post written for ONE specific avatar?
- [ ] Does it address where that avatar is in their buyer journey?
- [ ] Is the content 80% value, not 80% pitch?
- [ ] Is there a clear, specific CTA at the end?
- [ ] If educational: does it weave in a client story ("bridge technique")?
- [ ] Does it avoid sending traffic to another platform via a link?
- [ ] Is it focused on one single topic?
