# LinkedIn Post Guidebook

> Full post writing cycle: from idea to publishing. Focused on LinkedIn 2026 specifics.

---

## 1. Topic & Angle

Pick **one specific idea** — not "content marketing" but "why your LinkedIn posts don't get read past the first line."

**Content mix (70-20-10):**
- 70% — educational: frameworks, insights, how-to
- 20% — personal: stories, lessons, behind-the-scenes
- 10% — promotional: announcements, offers, CTAs

**Angles that work:**
- Contrarian: "Stop doing X"
- Open Loop: "I tried X for 30 days. Here's what happened."
- Costly Mistake: "This mistake cost me Y"
- Belief Shift: "I used to think X. Then Z happened."
- Data Hook: "3.2× more reach. Here's the framework."

---

## 2. Core Message

Write your thesis in one sentence before you start:

> _The reader should walk away with one clear idea they can retell to a colleague._

---

## 3. Post Structure

### Hook — first 210 characters (≈ 1–2 lines)
**This is everything the reader sees before "See more."**
- A specific number or fact
- A provocative statement or question
- Never start with "Excited to share…" or "Happy to announce…"

### Body
- Short paragraphs: 1–3 lines, then a blank line
- One paragraph — one idea
- Use bullets or numbered lists for 3+ items
- **Bold** — no more than once per paragraph

### CTA — last 2–3 lines
A specific question, not just "Follow me":
- "What would you add?"
- "Has this happened in your team?"
- "How do you handle this?"

---

## 4. Length

| Post Type | Length |
|---|---|
| Text-only | 1,200–1,500 characters (200–250 words) |
| Image post | 600–900 characters |
| Thought leadership | 1,000–1,500 characters |
| Personal story | 700–1,200 characters |

> Long-form posts (1,200–1,800 words) get 3.2× more reach — but only with a strong hook and clear structure.

---

## 5. Format

Choose based on the goal:

| Format | Best For |
|---|---|
| Text-only | Opinions, stories, conversations |
| Carousel (6–9 slides) | Frameworks, step-by-step guides |
| Native video (30–90 sec) | Demos, reactions, behind-the-scenes |
| Image | Quotes with context, data, screenshots |
| Poll | Quick engagement, audience research |

**Carousels:** one idea per slide, ≤12 words per slide, hook on slide 1, CTA on the last slide.

---

## 6. Hashtags

- **3–5 hashtags** — optimal
- More than 5 reduces algorithmic reach
- Use specific over broad (#reactjs not #programming)
- Place at the end of the post, not in the body

---

## 7. Timing

- **Best time:** Tuesday and Thursday, 10:00–11:00 AM (local time)
- **Working range:** Mon–Fri, 7:00 AM–4:00 PM
- **Frequency:** 3–5 posts per week (no more than 1 per day)

**The 90-Minute Rule:** most of your reach is determined in the first 90 minutes after publishing.

**+20% hack:** actively comment on others' posts 15–30 minutes before and after publishing your own.

---

## 8. After Publishing

- Reply to **every comment within 1–2 hours**
- Comments longer than 10 words are the strongest algorithm signal
- Do not delete or edit the post in the first few hours

---

## 9. What to Avoid

| ❌ Don't | Why |
|---|---|
| Engagement bait ("Like if you agree") | Algorithm suppresses it |
| 6+ hashtags | Reduced reach |
| Posting more than once a day | Audience fatigue, self-cannibalization |
| Links in the post body | LinkedIn lowers reach (put links in the first comment) |
| Fully AI-generated text without personal experience | Less distribution |

---

## Pre-Publish Checklist

- [ ] Hook ≤ 210 characters — does it stop the scroll?
- [ ] One core idea for the entire post
- [ ] Paragraphs ≤ 3 lines with blank lines between them
- [ ] Specific CTA with a question at the end
- [ ] 3–5 relevant hashtags
- [ ] No links in the body (if needed — put in first comment)
- [ ] Timing: Tue–Thu, 10–11 AM
