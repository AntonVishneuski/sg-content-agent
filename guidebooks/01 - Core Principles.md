## Principle 1: The 80/20 Rule

Never flood your audience with sales pitches. The proven breakdown:

- **80%** of all posts → pure value: tips, education, inspiration, entertainment
- **20%** of all posts → promotional content about your offer

A more granular version — the **50/30/20 Rule**:

| Split | Content Type |
|-------|-------------|
| 50% | Engaging and educational content you create |
| 30% | Curated industry content from other sources |
| 20% | Promotional content about your product or service |

> **Why it matters**: Audiences disengage when they feel sold to constantly. Value-first content earns the permission to sell.

---

## Principle 2: The Know → Like → Trust Factor

Think of your content strategy as **dating**, not marriage proposals.

```
Content → Audience gets to KNOW you
Consistency → Audience starts to LIKE you
Results/Stories → Audience begins to TRUST you
CTA → Now they're ready to BUY
```

- A single funny reel will not convert someone into a paying client.
- Trust is built through **long-form content** or **consistent value over time**.
- Never ask for the "marriage" (sale) before you've established the relationship.

---

## Principle 3: Media Over Social

Modern algorithms are **media matching engines**, not social networks.

> The platform's job is to connect your content with the right viewer. Your job is to make that matching easy.

**What this means in practice:**
- Be **militantly focused** on a single topic or niche
- Stay obsessively consistent about who you are talking to
- One account = one target avatar = one core problem you solve

Platforms reward specificity. Accounts that talk about everything get shown to no one. See [[03 - Audience & Targeting]] for the full avatar definition process.

---

## Principle 4: On-Target Virality

Chasing broad viral reach is a trap for business accounts.

| Wrong Goal | Right Goal |
|-----------|-----------|
| Millions of random views | Deep penetration within your ideal audience |
| Broad relatable content | Niche-specific pain-point content |
| "Freeloader" followers | Qualified leads who want what you sell |

**On-target virality** builds **cult fandom** within a narrow slice of your market. That fandom is more profitable than mass exposure.

---

## Principle 5: Intent Over Trend

Stop chasing trending audio and viral formats. Instead, **answer the questions your buyers are already asking**.

Before someone buys your service, they search for answers:
- *"How can I save time on X?"*
- *"What's the difference between A and B?"*
- *"Why isn't my Y working?"*

Write posts that intercept these questions. This creates content that attracts people in **buying or learning mode** — the highest-intent audience you can reach.

**Research tools mentioned:**
- TikTok Creator Search
- AnswerThePublic

## Summary

| Principle | One-Line Rule |
|-----------|--------------|
| 80/20 | Sell rarely, give often |
| Know-Like-Trust | Date before proposing |
| Media Over Social | Pick one topic, one avatar |
| On-Target Virality | Small focused audience > big random audience |
| Intent Over Trend | Answer buyer questions, don't chase trends |
