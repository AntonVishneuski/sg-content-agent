# Medium Article Guidebook

> Full article writing cycle: from idea to publishing. Focused on Medium 2026 specifics.

---

## What Makes Medium Different

Medium is a **publishing platform**, not a social network. What matters here:
- **Read ratio** (% of readers who finish) — the primary algorithm metric
- **Curation** — editorial selection that multiplies reach by 10–100×
- **Depth and originality** — surface-level content doesn't get curated

---

## 1. Topic & Angle

Choose a topic with a **specific focus**. Not "productivity" but "why morning routines don't work for engineers."

**What gets curated:**
- Original insight or personal experience
- Research backed by data or concrete examples
- A unique perspective on a known problem
- Actionable takeaways — the reader must walk away with something

**What gets rejected:**
- Recycled common advice
- Clickbait without depth
- Promotional content
- AI-generated text without substantial editing

---

## 2. Core Message

Write this before you start:

> _One thesis. One shift in the reader's thinking. One action they will take after reading._

---

## 3. Headline

The headline is the #1 factor for CTR. Test 5–10 variations before publishing.

**Formulas that work:**
- Specific result + timeframe: "How I Grew from 0 to 10K Followers in 6 Months"
- Number + benefit: "7 Mistakes That Kill Your Medium Read Ratio"
- Counterintuitive take: "Why Publishing More Often Destroys Your Authority"
- Pain-driven question: "Why Don't People Read Your Articles to the End?"

**Rules:**
- Specific numbers > abstract promises
- Avoid clickbait — curators will reject it
- The headline must accurately reflect the content

---

## 4. Article Structure

### Opening (200–300 words)
- **Lines 1–3:** a counterintuitive statement or surprising data point — the hook
- Establish context: why this matters to the reader personally
- Preview what the article covers and what the reader will gain
- Mention your experience or expertise — credibility

### Body (1,000–2,500 words)
Each section follows: **Insight → Evidence → Implication for the reader**

- 3–5 main sections with H2 headings
- New H2 every 300–500 words
- Paragraphs: 2–4 sentences maximum
- "Micro-hooks" every 3 paragraphs — one short sentence that creates curiosity
- Specific examples, data, and case studies — no generic statements

### Conclusion (150–200 words)
- Synthesize the key insights
- Provide actionable next steps
- End with a thought-provoking question or prediction that keeps readers thinking

---

## 5. Length

| Content Type | Length |
|---|---|
| Minimum for curation | 1,000+ words |
| General optimum | 1,200–2,000 words |
| Thought leadership | 2,000–3,000 words |
| Deep dive / research | 3,000+ words |

> Articles over 2,000 words get curated at 2× the rate of 1,000-word articles. But length without depth doesn't work.

---

## 6. Formatting

- **H2** — main sections
- **H3** — subsections
- **Bold** — key phrases, used sparingly
- Block quotes — for important insights
- Code blocks — for technical content
- Images: 1 per every 500 words
- Bullet/numbered lists — for scannable content
- Unbroken text walls = reader drop-off

---

## 7. Tags (Topics)

- Choose **3–5 specific tags** that accurately describe the content
- Don't pick broad tags for more reach — curators will reject mis-categorized articles
- Example: article about React → "React", "Web Development", "JavaScript" (not "Technology")
- Browse what gets curated in your target topic before submitting

---

## 8. Publications

- Submit to a relevant publication = wider distribution
- Read each publication's submission guidelines before pitching
- One article = one publication (exclusivity)
- Publications with active editors increase curation chances

---

## 9. Frequency & Consistency

- **Optimum:** 2–3 articles per week (or 1–2 high-quality pieces per month for thought leadership)
- Consistency matters more than volume
- Daily publishing dilutes quality and lowers curation chances

---

## 10. After Publishing

- Share on LinkedIn and other channels within the first 24 hours
- Reply to comments — engagement influences the algorithm
- Track **read ratio** (target: 40%+) and claps
- High read ratio articles get pushed into recommendations

---

## 11. What to Avoid

| ❌ Don't | Why |
|---|---|
| Republish content from other platforms | Violates guidelines, blocked from curation |
| Affiliate links in the first 3 paragraphs | Automatic rejection |
| Self-promotion without value | Curators reject it |
| Generic advice without specifics | Low read ratio |
| Inaccurate tags | Mis-categorization → rejection |

---

## Pre-Publish Checklist

- [ ] Headline: specific, with a number or clear benefit, no clickbait
- [ ] First 3 lines — hook with a counterintuitive claim or data
- [ ] Structure: H2 every 300–500 words
- [ ] Paragraphs: ≤ 4 sentences, blank lines between them
- [ ] Length: minimum 1,000 words (1,500+ preferred)
- [ ] Images: 1 per every 500 words
- [ ] 3–5 accurate tags that match the content
- [ ] Original insight or experience — nothing generic
- [ ] Conclusion with a specific question or call to action
- [ ] No affiliate links in the first 3 paragraphs
