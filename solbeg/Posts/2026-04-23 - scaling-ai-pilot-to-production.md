# Scaling AI from Pilot to Production — Why 74% of Enterprises Fail

78% of enterprises use AI. Only 24% have actually scaled it.

The gap isn't about technology — it's about what comes before the technology.

According to TEKsystems' State of Digital Transformation 2026 report, most organizations are stuck in the same loop: launch a pilot, see promising results, try to scale, hit a wall. MIT's GenAI Divide study confirms it — the majority of AI pilots never make it to enterprise-wide execution.

Here's what we keep seeing block the path from pilot to production:

**1. Wrong success metrics.**
Teams measure how fast AI generates output, not whether business outcomes actually improved. Speed without direction scales noise.

**2. The foundation wasn't built for scale.**
You can't bolt AI onto a fragmented monolith and expect it to hold. Governance, data quality, and architecture have to come first — not after.

**3. Pilots are optimized in isolation.**
A proof-of-concept lives in controlled conditions. The enterprise doesn't. When the real environment arrives — legacy integrations, compliance requirements, cross-team dependencies — the pilot breaks.

We worked through exactly this with a 54-location automotive group managing $440M+ in annual revenue. The priority wasn't new tools. It was rebuilding the foundation: legacy system modernization, CI/CD pipelines for zero-downtime deployments, proper data architecture.

Once the base was right, everything scaled — +200% website traffic YoY, 18% of total revenue moved online, −22% missed service appointments.

The lesson: transformation doesn't fail at the pilot stage. It fails when organizations try to scale something that was never designed to scale.
