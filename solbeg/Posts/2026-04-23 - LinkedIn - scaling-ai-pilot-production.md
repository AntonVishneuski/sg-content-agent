# Scaling AI from Pilot to Production — LinkedIn Post

78% of enterprises use AI. Only 24% have actually scaled it.

The gap isn't about technology.

After working on digital transformation projects — from legacy modernization to full omnichannel platforms — we keep seeing the same three blockers:

**1. Wrong success metric.**
Teams measure output speed, not business outcomes. Faster AI ≠ faster results.

**2. Unstable foundation.**
You can't layer AI onto a fragmented monolith. Architecture, governance, and data quality have to come first — not after.

**3. Pilots live in a bubble.**
A PoC works in controlled conditions. The real enterprise doesn't. Legacy integrations, compliance requirements, cross-team dependencies — the pilot breaks.

We saw this directly with a 54-location automotive group managing $440M+ in annual revenue.

The goal wasn't new tools. It was fixing the foundation first: legacy systems modernized, CI/CD pipelines built, data architecture cleaned up.

Once that was right, everything scaled — +200% website traffic YoY, 18% of revenue moved online, −22% missed service appointments.

Transformation doesn't fail at the pilot. It fails when you try to scale something that was never built to scale.
