# Agentic Software Development & Harness Engineering

Your AI agent just filed a PR at 2am.
It passed CI. It looks clean. It might be completely wrong.

Agentic coding tools are everywhere in 2026. Agents that read your full repo, decompose tasks, write code, fix failing tests, and submit pull requests — without anyone at the keyboard.

The early numbers are hard to ignore: PR cycle times compressed by 87%. Boilerplate work cut in half. Features moving from spec to review in under 30 minutes (Gagan Deep Singh, Medium).

But Thoughtworks flagged something critical in their April 2026 Technology Radar: the industry isn't hitting an adoption wall. It's hitting a **reliability** wall.

They call it "harness engineering" — the emerging discipline of making agentic AI predictable, auditable, and safe in production.

What it actually means in practice:
→ Measure iteration cycles per task — not just code throughput
→ Track post-merge rework and failed builds — not PR volume
→ Define hard boundaries on what agents can and cannot touch
→ Build deliberate human checkpoints into autonomous workflows

The tools aren't the bottleneck. The guardrails are.

We've seen this on our own delivery teams. The engineering fundamentals that make agentic AI trustworthy — CI/CD pipelines, automated testing coverage, clear architectural scope — are the same ones we've been building into client projects for years. The teams that ship reliably with AI agents are the ones that already had the structure in place.
